## 📖 About This Book

> Over the years in the internet industry, I've worked in design and product, driven hundreds of millions in revenue growth, and also optimized experiences so small you'd need a magnifying glass to see them. I wrote this book because I've seen too many newcomers — including my younger self — work tirelessly on skills, only to hit walls due to a lack of big-picture thinking. If that young person back then had these insights into how industries work, he could have made much bigger waves.

This is an **industry cognition guide** for workplace newcomers. Through 16 stories of a young person on Coral Island, it reveals how to understand the underlying logic of any industry.

**No dry theories, no complex models — just vivid stories and verifiable real-world cases.**

## 🎯 Who Is This For?

- 🌱 New professionals with 1-3 years of experience
- 🔄 People considering a career change but unsure how to evaluate industries
- 💪 Those who feel they work hard but can't see the big picture
- 🧠 Product managers, operators, and entrepreneurs who want to build an industry thinking framework

### Lesson 1: There Are Only Two Reasons People Pay

> When users spend money, it ultimately comes down to two reasons: either you **actually solved their problem**, or you made them **feel like the problem was solved**.

**Real-world case**: Bottled coffee at ¥15 vs. Starbucks pour-over at ¥38

### Lesson 13: Minimum Validation

> **Near-zero-cost validation should always happen before spending money.** A fake landing page, a three-minute video, ten cups of sample coffee — these could save you a year of detours.

**Real-world case**: Dropbox validated demand with a 3-minute video; Zappos started by photographing shoes in stores
